# Founder Exit Math Dashboard — Product Spec v0.1

## Audience

Tech founders who need plain-English understanding of ownership dilution, investor payback rights, payment order, and personal sale outcomes.

## Product rule

The product must read like a founder guide, not an internal software tool. The user-facing app must avoid developer-facing language.

## Core promise

Show how a founder can own meaningful equity but still receive $0 if debts, fees, investor payback rights, payment order, or double-payment terms consume sale proceeds first.

## Required user flow

1. Choose a starting example.
2. Enter ownership today.
3. Add funding rounds and investor rights.
4. Add debts, fees, and sale costs.
5. Choose possible sale prices.
6. See the payment order.
7. See what the founder may actually receive.
8. Compare different deal paths.

## Built-in examples

- Clean Seed Round
- Heavy VC Stack
- Investor Gets Paid Twice
- Founder Walks Away With $0
- What Sale Price Do I Need?
- Down Round Pressure
- Debt Before Equity

## Temporary saved scenarios

Users can save edited scenarios while the page is open. These saved scenarios are temporary and disappear on refresh. This is intentional. There is no login, account, database, or permanent saved workspace in v0.1.

## Required warnings

- Educational scenario simulator only.
- Not legal, tax, accounting, financial, or investment advice.
- Real term sheets can include additional rights that change outcomes.
- A founder can own shares and still receive $0.

## Validation standard

The math and UX copy must be validated before delivery. Required checks cover payment order, investor payback, participation, capped participation, debt before equity, founder $0 outcomes, first-dollar thresholds, resettable examples, temporary saved scenarios, and absence of internal/developer language in visible UI.
